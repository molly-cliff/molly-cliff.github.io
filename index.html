About Me

I’m Molly, a second-year PhD student in the Vet Med department at the University of Cambridge. My research uses statistical and geospatial methods to investigate the potential impact of climate change on the distribution of bacterial meningitis across Africa.

Before starting my PhD I worked as an Epidemiology and Information Analyst within the UK Health Security Agency Field Service on a wide variety of communicable disease outbreaks. Through this I undertook a secondment with the WHO working as an epidemiologist on the monkeypox outbreak within Europe. I hope to use my background in infectious disease epidemiology to support global public health programs, focusing on integrating research into practice.

Outside of research, I enjoy rock climbing, making art, and listening to music.

Research

My PhD project investigates the potential impact of climate change on the distribution and incidence of bacterial meningitis, focusing on meningococcal meningitis across Africa. As part of this, I have developed a predictive geographic model that estimates meningitis outbreak risk across Africa using temporal climate variables, updating existing risk mapping. Building on this, I am now using R-INLA to evaluate this relationship over space and time both at a country and continental level. I am aiming to use the models I am currently developing to estimate the future risk of meningitis outbreaks across Africa using General Circulation Models (GCMs) across different climatic scenarios.
