About Me

I’m Molly, a second-year PhD student in the Vet Med department at the University of Cambridge. My research uses statistical and geospatial methods to investigate the potential impact of climate change on the distribution of bacterial meningitis across Africa. Before starting my PhD I worked as an Epidemiology and Information Analyst within the UK Health Security Agency Field Service on a wide variety of communicable disease outbreaks. Through this I undertook a secondment with the WHO working as an epidemiologist on the monkeypox outbreak within Europe. I hope to use my background in infectious disease epidemiology to support global public health programs, focusing on integrating research into practice.

Outside of research, I enjoy rock climbing, making art, and listening to music.

Research

My work explores how environmental and climatic factors shape infectious disease dynamics, particularly bacterial meningitis across Africa.
